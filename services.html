    • Organic Framing

    Organic farming is a method of agriculture that focuses on producing crops and livestock without the use of synthetic chemicals, such as pesticides, herbicides, and fertilizers, or genetically modified organisms (GMOs).

    Chemical Farming

    Chemical farming uses synthetic chemicals like fertilizers, pesticides, and herbicides to boost crop growth and control pests. It's short-term, but can lead to soil degradation, water contamination,and long-term pest resistance.

    Check

    Crop Monitoring

    Crop monitoring, autonomous farming, and vertical farming utilize advanced technologies to monitor crop health, automate tasks, and reduce labor costs, enhancing efficiency and productivity.

    Check

    HiTech Farming

    High-tech farming, also known as precision agriculture, uses advanced technologies to optimize crop yields, reduce waste, and promote sustainability, including precision irrigation systems using sensors, drones, and satellite imaging.
